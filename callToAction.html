Duration of Sleep is Linked to Mental Health in Different Ways for Boys and Girls

It is no question that sleep affects people's daily lives. Getting little sleep makes a person feel fatigued and less energetic throughout the day.

Not getting the right amount of sleep affects not only your physical health, but also your mental health and abilities. Because of this, it is important that you get enough sleep each night to protect your overall well-being.

This study that was published by BMC Public Health, where common mental disorders (CMDs) using the General Health Questionaire at the beginning of a 9-month period and a follow-up.

This information suggests that there is a link between amount of sleep and mental health, as there was only a minor change in CMD scores with adequate amounts of sleep, and great changes with short or long sleep for both genders.

Also, note that the typical CMD scores for girls are typically lower than those for boys, showing that boys may have higher tendencies to have common mental disorders (though there is evidence that the opposite is true for adults).

Using this information, we can conclude that getting a different amount of sleep than what is recommended can lead to greater changes in mental health scores than getting an adequate amount of sleep.
