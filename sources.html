I got two of my sources from statista, from various datasets about sleep and mental health. Some are surveys put out by statista, while others are taken from the CDC or other health organizations (SAMHSA; NSDUH). My other source was from the BMC Public Health webpage, which I trust is a valid source.

Source 1

SAMHSA. (September 11, 2020). Percentage of U.S. adults with any mental illness in the past year as of 2019, by age and gender [Graph]. In Statista. Retrieved October 08, 2020, from https://www.statista.com/statistics/252311/mental-illness-in-the-past-year-among-us-adults-by-age-and-gender/

This source has data about the percentages of American adults that have mental illnesses based on gender and age.

Source 2

Statista Survey. (February 2, 2017). Percentage of adults in the U.S. who reported they get enough sleep as of January 2017, by gender [Graph]. In Statista. Retrieved October 07, 2020, from https://www.statista.com/statistics/668101/sleep-sufficiency-among-us-adults-gender/

This source has data about the percentages of American adults that get enough sleep by gender.
